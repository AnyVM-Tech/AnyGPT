    Scanning policy

    Observational-only public posture

    The public-facing scanner is intended to measure exposed services and metadata without creating, updating, deleting, or authenticating into target systems.

    • No login attempts, brute-force behavior, or authenticated entry paths.
    • No create, update, delete, or other state-changing interactions.
    • Measurement is constrained by explicit throttles and host-level backoff.
    • Owners can claim assets, request opt-out, or file abuse reports below.

    Owner workflows

    Open a panel only when you need it

    Claim ownership, request opt-out, or file an abuse report without wading through a wall of forms.

    Claim ownership Verify control of a domain, IP, or CIDR.

    `dns_txt` checks for an exact token in a TXT record on the claimed domain. `http_file` checks for the token at /.well-known/anyscan-verification.txt on the claimed host. `email` and `manual_review` are recorded for operator review.

    Request opt-out Ask for public measurement to stop after verification.

    Automated verification is available for `dns_txt` and `http_file`. Email-based requests are accepted, but ownership still needs manual operator confirmation before the workflow can be marked verified.

    Report abuse or complaints Escalate urgent issues tied to measurement behavior.

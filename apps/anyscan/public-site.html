AnyScan

This service is positioned as a public internet measurement and exposure-notification surface. It is intended to document scanner behavior, provide owner controls, publish disclosure contact details, and accept opt-out and abuse submissions without exposing the internal operator control plane.

Scanning policy

The public-facing scanner is intended to remain observational. It documents exposed services and metadata while avoiding state-changing access patterns and authenticated request profiles.

  • No login attempts, brute-force behavior, database reads, or authenticated entry paths.
  • No create, update, delete, or other state-changing operations against target systems.
  • Measurement is constrained by explicit runtime throttles and host-level backoff.
  • Resource owners can claim assets, request opt-out, or report abuse through the workflows below.
